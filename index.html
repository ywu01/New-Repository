BIO

I'm a senior at Harvard majoring in Economics and minoring in Computer Science. I interned at Zinc my sophomore summer and emberlight the summer after that. During junior year, I worked with Daniel Wu and a few others on a President's Challenge entry, WelcomeHome, which made it to the final round. I thought that was pretty cool.

Besides the tech/startup environment, I've also been a photographer for Kirkland House for the past two years. That originally overlaped with a multimedia stint at The Harvard Crimson, but I have since settled for a more flexible role at the House.

I'm interested in the intricacies of great products, and how they're designed, built, and released into the wild. My resume offers some additional details about that and my previous work.

PROJECTS/EXPERIENCE

KIRKLAND HOUSE.I've been the house photographer for the past three years. Some of them are included in my portfolio, accessible below.

EMBERLIGHT.Emberlight is an internet-of-things startup that grew out of a 300k Kickstarter campaign. In contrast to the smaller role I took at Zinc, I ran most of the marketing operations/content production for the two months I was present. Beyond marketing, I also assisted with some operations related tasks coming out from Amazon.

WELCOMEHOME.An entry into the Harvard President's Challenge, WelcomeHome was a question about the viability of microliving in large, urban US cities. My role involved a lot of value proposition validation, spanning from interviews inside university dining halls to landing page mockups. We ended up with finalist status. We also ended up with semi-finalist status on MIT100k.

THE CRIMSON.I was an executive for the multimedia board for a year, and now participate casually as a JE. I've had a few hundred of photos published for this student-run publication throughout my three years there. Pageviews run in the millions annually for the website; beyond photos, I've previously worked on a HTML/CSS implementation of a blockquote format in liquid.

ZINC.Zinc is an e-commerce startup that came out of YC's Winter 2015 batch. As a business analyst, I did a mix of business development, marketing, sales, and support. This was my first internship, and I took it in as a good introduction to business roles in startups. zinc.io

DATA VISUALIZATION.The final project for CS171, a course about data visualization. This particular visualization draws data from the Spotify API, and creates a visual network of artists based on an initial query. Double clicking nodes will lead you to the artist at that particular node; using the toolbar in the middle will also allow you to shuffle, play different songs, and navigate. Worked in a team of three. Hosted on Github.
